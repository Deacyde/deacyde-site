Step-by-Step Setup

Do these in order — some steps require a reboot

1

Power Off the Pi Completely

Make sure the Pi is fully shut down and the power cord is unplugged before touching anything. Not just restarted — fully off and unplugged.

⚠️ Never attach or remove the HAT, SSD, or FPC cable while the Pi is powered on.
2

Seat the Samsung NVMe into the M.2 HAT+

Hold the HAT board. You'll see the M-key M.2 slot (the longer notched slot). Insert the Samsung NVMe at a slight angle (~30°), slide it in all the way until it's fully seated, then press it flat down.

  • Secure it with the included standoff screw — don't overtighten, just snug
  • The SSD should lie flat and not wiggle
💡 The Samsung NVMe is M-key — it only fits one way. If it doesn't slide in cleanly, check the notch alignment.
3

Connect the FPC Ribbon Cable

This flat ribbon cable connects the HAT to the Pi 5's PCIe slot. It's the most important and most fragile part of the build.

  • Both ends have a locking tab — lift it up gently before inserting
  • Slide the cable all the way into the connector (gold contacts face down)
  • Press the locking tab down firmly to secure — you should feel/hear a click
  • Do both ends — the HAT end and the Pi 5 board end
⚠️ This cable is the #1 cause of NVMe not being detected. If the Pi can't find the drive after boot, this is the first thing to reseat. Make sure both tabs are fully locked and the cable is flush.
4

Stack the M.2 HAT+ onto the Pi 5

The HAT connects to the Pi via the 40-pin GPIO header on the top of the Pi board.

  • Line up the HAT's header pins over the Pi's GPIO header
  • Press down firmly and evenly — all 40 pins need to seat at once
  • The HAT should sit flush with no gap on either side
  • Use the included standoffs to secure the HAT to the Pi board (prevents flex)
5

Fit the Pi + HAT into the Argon NEO Case

The Argon NEO 5 M.2 case is designed for exactly this sandwich — Pi on top, HAT underneath. The SSD slot in the case base lines up with the NVMe on the HAT.

  • Lower the Pi + HAT assembly into the case base, HAT facing down
  • Line up the ports (USB, HDMI, Ethernet) with the cutouts on the case
  • Press the assembly down into the case until it sits flush
  • Screw the board into the case standoffs (usually 4 screws)
6

Connect the Fan Header

The Argon NEO has a small fan. There's a JST fan connector on the Pi 5 board — connect the fan cable to it before closing the case.

  • The Pi 5 fan connector is a small 2-pin or 4-pin JST on the board near the USB ports
  • The Argon fan cable plugs straight in — only goes one way
  • Make sure the cable is tucked and won't catch on the case lid
💡 Without the fan daemon software (set up by Copilot later), the fan will either run full blast or not at all. That's fine for now.
7

Close the Case

  • Place the case lid on top
  • Secure with the included screws — usually 4 screws on the corners or sides
  • Don't overtighten — the case is aluminum and threads strip easily
  • Give the assembled unit a gentle shake — nothing should rattle
8

Plug In & Hand Off to Copilot

Hardware is done. Plug in the official Pi 5 27W USB-C power supply and let Copilot handle the rest:

  • EEPROM firmware update
  • PCIe boot order configuration
  • OS flash to NVMe
  • Argon fan daemon install
  • SSH, hostname, and software setup
💡 If nothing appears on screen at first — that's expected if there's no OS on the NVMe yet. Copilot will walk through flashing it.

Hardware Troubleshooting

Physical issues only — software problems handled by Copilot

🔌

NVMe not detected at all

Almost always the FPC ribbon cable. Power off, unplug, open the case, and reseat both ends of the flat cable. Lift the tab, push cable fully in, press tab down until it clicks. Also check the SSD standoff screw is snug.

📡

HAT feels loose or wobbly

The 40-pin GPIO header wasn't fully pressed down. Power off, remove the HAT, line up the pins carefully, and press down firmly and evenly in one go. Use the HAT's standoff screws to lock it to the Pi board.

💾

SSD not sitting flat

The NVMe wasn't inserted at the right angle. Remove the standoff screw, pull the SSD out, re-insert at ~30° until fully seated in the slot, then press flat and re-secure the screw. It should lie completely flat with no flex.

🌡️

Fan not spinning

Hardware-side: check that the fan JST connector is fully plugged into the Pi 5's fan port. It's a small connector near the USB ports — it should click in. If connected and still not spinning, that's a software/daemon config issue for Copilot.

🔧

Case won't close flush

Usually the FPC cable is bunched up or a standoff isn't seated right. Make sure the ribbon cable is routed flat and not folded. Check all 4 board standoffs are in before trying to seat the lid.

⚡

Pi powers on but nothing on screen

Hardware check: confirm the HDMI cable is in the HDMI 0 port (the one closest to the USB-C power port). The Pi 5 requires HDMI 0 for initial output. This is a hardware/port issue — not software.
